Discography • Albums & Backstories

About Paramore

Paramore is an American rock band formed in 2004 in Tennessee. They became one of the most influential modern rock bands, known for emotional lyrics, powerful vocals from Hayley Williams, and a mix of pop-punk and alternative rock sounds.

All We Know Is Falling (2005)

This debut album was created during a very emotional time for the band. It reflects loss, transition, and finding identity. The music is raw and emotional, with early pop-punk energy. Songs like “Pressure” and “Emergency” show teenage frustration and vulnerability.

Trivia: The empty couch represents absence and loss.

Riot! (2007)

Riot! is Paramore’s breakthrough album that brought them worldwide fame. It captures teenage emotions like anger, love, confusion, and rebellion. The sound is more powerful and polished, with iconic tracks like “Misery Business” and “That’s What You Get.”

Trivia: The title represents emotional chaos and intensity.

Brand New Eyes (2009)

This album shows a more mature and reflective Paramore. It was created during internal struggles within the band, focusing on communication, conflict, and emotional growth. Songs like “The Only Exception” and “Ignorance” became fan favorites.

Trivia: The butterfly represents transformation and rebirth.
